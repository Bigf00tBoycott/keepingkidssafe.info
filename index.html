The Invisible Threat is Real

The internet is an incredible tool for learning and connection, but it also harbors hidden dangers. Child predators are sophisticated and use online games, social media, and chat rooms to groom and exploit children. Our mission is to shine a light on these threats and equip you with strategies to keep your family safe.

Recognize the Dangers

Awareness is the first step toward protection.

Online Predators

Individuals who use the internet to establish emotional connections with children for the purpose of sexual abuse and exploitation. They often hide their true identity and intentions.

Cyberbullying

The use of digital communication tools to harass, threaten, or humiliate someone. It can happen 24/7 and is often anonymous, causing significant emotional distress.

Inappropriate Content

Exposure to violent, pornographic, or hateful material that is not age-appropriate and can be harmful to a child's psychological development.

Practical Safety Tips

Simple steps can make a world of difference.

Communicate Openly

Create a safe space for your child to talk about their online experiences without fear of judgment or punishment. Ask them about the games and apps they use.

Set Clear Rules & Boundaries

Establish rules for screen time, websites they can visit, and the kind of information they can share online. Keep computers in a central family area.

Emphasize Privacy

Teach children never to share personal information like their full name, address, phone number, school name, or passwords online.

Use Parental Controls

Utilize software and device settings to filter inappropriate content, limit screen time, and monitor online activity. These are helpful tools, but not a substitute for communication.
